### Summary

The onboarding and strategy meeting, attended by Jason Bagley, Lauren Peacock, Taylor Lawrence, Bjorn, and Dean Nixon, established EBox as a distinct, startup-like entity within Open Box focused on solving real estate software gaps, particularly in the MRI ERP system, with their E-forms and E-Connect products. Jason Bagley outlined the engagement approach to identify EBox's current status and goals, which include growing their Annual Recurring Revenue from 42 unique clients and transitioning branding from "openbox software" to "eBox" to aggressively market and conduct outreach to their list of MRI PMXD clients. Bjorn and Dean Nixon agreed to prioritize an urgent website build by Jason Bagley's team using Ripplet, a sales process workshop, and a marketing outreach strategy, leveraging the current client base and addressing common sales friction like requests for SOC compliance.

### Details

* **Meeting Attendees and Purpose** Jason Bagley initiated the meeting, which included Lauren Peacock, Taylor Lawrence, Bjorn, and Dean Nixon, after confirming Taylor's acceptance to the call. The purpose of the meeting was to conduct onboarding, where Jason would lead the process, and the rest of the team would take notes and jump in with questions ([00:00:00](#00:00:00)) ([00:07:40](#00:07:40)). Bjorn was identified as the Director of Products at EBox, and Dean Nixon was the CTO ([00:08:36](#00:08:36)).

* **EBox Business Structure and Team Introductions** Bjorn and Dean Nixon clarified that EBox operates as a distinct entity, despite being within the Open Box group, functioning like a startup ([00:08:36](#00:08:36)). Jason Bagley introduced themself as the main point of contact from Growth Experts, working alongside Taylor Lawrence, who specializes in copywriting, data, and analytics ([00:07:40](#00:07:40)). Lauren Peacock supports everything else in the background ([00:08:36](#00:08:36)). * **Engagement Strategy with EBox** Jason Bagley outlined the engagement approach, which involves uncovering EBox's current situation (Point A) and defining their vision and goals (Point B). The Growth Experts team will focus on strategy and execution, including running tests, measuring results, ensuring correct tracking, learning from data, and repeating successful processes to help EBox reach its goals ([00:10:51](#00:10:51)).

* **Origin and Market Gap for EBox Products** Dean Nixon explained that EBox exists to fulfill gaps in the MRI software ERP system, which they primarily support, stemming from client demand. Initially developing e-connect for one client, they expanded to their current client base ([00:12:44](#00:12:44)). Bjorn added that the broader purpose is to create software that solves gaps in real estate solutions generally, though currently focused on MRI ([00:14:06](#00:14:06)).

* **E-Forms Product and Problem Solved** E-forms was created to address the lack of a user-friendly way to import data into MRI's ERP system. Dean Nixon noted that MRI's existing solutions for data import are often uncomfortable for clients and lack a proper solution, despite having shown a potential solution years ago that has not progressed ([00:14:06](#00:14:06)). E-forms is positioned as a user-friendly, business-centric import solution ([00:15:35](#00:15:35)).

* **Competitive Landscape and Acquisition Strategy** Dean Nixon and Bjorn discussed MRI's focus on acquiring companies rather than investing in existing products, leading to technical debt ([00:16:50](#00:16:50)). MRI has an in-house competitor tool for data import, but it still lacks necessary user considerations ([00:24:23](#00:24:23)). Bjorn confirmed EBox is focused on creating a product business with longevity and is not actively seeking acquisition of the entire entity ([00:19:00](#00:19:00)).

* **E-Forms Core Functionality and Differentiation** Dean Nixon described e-forms as allowing the crafting of imports to reflect specific business scenarios, such as accounting journals or lease data, enabling bulk data processing ([00:20:11](#00:20:11)). Bjorn likened e-forms to a "Google forms for real estate software," highly customizable with the addition of workflows for approvals and notifications. MRI's existing method for data import is an admin-centric, CSV-based tool that requires users to understand underlying database tables and poses high risks, such as the ability to delete data ([00:22:17](#00:22:17)).

* **E-Connect Product and Value Proposition** E-Connect was developed to solve user frustrations with emailing correspondence to tenants through MRI, which offered limited branding, attachment control, and tracking ([00:30:10](#00:30:10)). E-Connect uses the client's own email domains, tracks delivery, allows securing (encrypting) documents, and branding the entire email chain to reflect the client's brand ([00:31:27](#00:31:27)). Bjorn demonstrated the stark difference in customer experience between MRI's generic emails and the custom, professional emails facilitated by E-Connect, calling the transition a "huge kind of we've gotten here once with a client" that secured a sale quickly ([00:33:37](#00:33:37)).

* **MRI Relationship and Pricing Models** Dean Nixon described the relationship with MRI as "very good," especially regarding E-Connect, though E-forms is newer to market ([00:25:25](#00:25:25)). Bjorn added that EBox are official partners with MRI, but E-Connect is listed on their partner page, not E-forms ([00:26:59](#00:26:59)). MRI's issues, such as poor email design, are likely due to focusing on legislative changes and technical debt rather than revenue generation from fixing these problems ([00:37:00](#00:37:00)). E-Connect is priced based on lease volumes, while E-forms is based on user volume, with both generally billed annually in advance ([00:38:58](#00:38:58)).

* **Ideal Customer Profile and Target Market** The ideal customer for EBox, particularly for E-forms, should be using MRI software, preferably with a minimum of 300 to 400 plus leases ([00:40:01](#00:40:01)). The sweet spot includes commercial real estate, mixed-use, or retail sectors, as residential properties often have smaller budgets despite high unit numbers. The initial focus for E-forms is on North America and the MRI PMX product ([00:41:11](#00:41:11)). The main problem areas addressed by E-forms are accounting journals, leasing, and vendor/accounts payable use cases. Target roles for decision-makers include Head of Finance, CFO, or Head of Asset Management/Property Manager ([00:43:20](#00:43:20)) ([00:45:38](#00:45:38)).

* **EBox Current Status and Goals** EBox currently has a team of 11 people and 42 unique clients across their two core products ([00:47:43](#00:47:43)). Their billing is annual, with only consulting work being charged monthly, which currently accounts for about 10% of revenue ([00:48:55](#00:48:55)). EBox is focused on growing its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) and is willing to sacrifice consulting work to secure licenses. They have about 30 open leads, with two hot leads for e-forms ([00:49:56](#00:49:56)). Bjorn is confident that getting an initial sales pitch to 80 clients in North America would be a significant step toward their revenue goals for the next 12 months ([00:54:50](#00:54:50)).

* **Technology Stack and Sales Process** EBox primarily uses Pipe Drive for sales tracking, Office 365, Loom for recording demos, and Zendesk for support, but they are flexible with Loom and Zendesk ([00:55:46](#00:55:46)) ([00:57:28](#00:57:28)). Bjorn noted that the sales data in Pipe Drive is improving ([00:56:32](#00:56:32)). The sales process is managed formally by Bjorn and Dean Nixon, with some support from individuals across the Open Box group for introductions and initial assessments ([00:58:27](#00:58:27)).

* **Sales Objections and Friction Points** Taylor Lawrence asked about common objections during the sales process. Bjorn stated that there are no frequent "nos" but some friction points include requests for SOC compliance, which EBox addresses with ISO 27001 certification, and regional hosting in America, which they are prepared to address if a large client requires it. Smaller clients, particularly in APAC and South Africa, sometimes find the price too high ([00:59:18](#00:59:18)).

* **Past Growth Efforts and Learning** Jason Bagley initiated the discussion by asking about past growth attempts, successes, and failures, as well as current activities ([01:01:40](#01:01:40)). Bjorn mentioned that attending conferences under the "open box" consultancy banner, particularly in America, was a failure because they lacked the attention, traction, and access typically afforded to a product company ([01:02:25](#01:02:25)). Dean Nixon added that the messaging was lost in the noise, leading people not to realize they had a solution for a specific gap ([01:03:24](#01:03:24)).

* **Topup's US Marketing and Product Focus** Dean Nixon discussed unsuccessful marketing efforts for "econ" into the US with Topup, the entity Open Box acquired software from, where Dean Nixon used to be the CTO ([01:03:24](#01:03:24)) ([01:05:31](#01:05:31)). * **Current Sales and Team Strategy** Bjorn explained that they are aiming for lean team growth, targeting to only increase the team by 20% even if revenue doubles, focusing on good processes and good people for exponential growth ([01:05:31](#01:05:31)). Bjorn highlighted that a successful current practice for econ sales is utilizing simple visualizations and friction-focused selling instead of a full sales demo of the product, which is often too detailed for decision-makers, resulting in most signatures without the client ever seeing the actual product ([01:06:20](#01:06:20)).

* **Client Communication and Conferences** Dean Nixon reiterated that the main focus should be the website and the outreach to the MRI list, alongside leveraging the existing client base for cross-selling e-forms to current e-connect users ([01:21:02](#01:21:02)). Dean Nixon also mentioned two upcoming MRI-based conferences, one in Australia (August) and a large one in America (October).
